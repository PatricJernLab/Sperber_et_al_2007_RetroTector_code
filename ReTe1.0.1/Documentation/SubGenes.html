RetroTector

 

The file SubGenes.txt


This parameter file in Database subdirectories specifies SubGenes and the allowed distances between them.

A SubGene is a collection of MotifGroups from the same major component of the retrovirus. A SubGene hit is composed of one or more Motif hits and has a hotspot. The distances between the Motif hit hotspots and the SubGene hit hotspot must conform with the contraints in MotifGroups.txt.

SubGene hits are combined into Chains, representing possible retroviruses. The distances between the SubGene hits are expected (though there are exceptions) to conform to the constraints contained in SubGenes.txt.

SubGenes.txt is preferably but not necessarily formatted into columns, so that the correspondence between fields in the lines is clear.
It contains a number of singleparameters:

  • One with tke key 'Names'. Its contents part is a blank-delimited list of names of all the SubGenes.
  • As many singleparameters as there are SubGenes. The key of each is the name of a SubGene. Its contents part is a blank-delimited list of range specifiers, specifying the acceptable range of distances between the hotspot of a hit by the SubGene specified by the key, and the hotspot of a hit by the SubGene named at the same position in the 'Names' parameter. (This sounds awful but is much clearer if you look into the file).
