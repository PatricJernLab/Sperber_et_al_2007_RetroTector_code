A Chain file may contain more than one chain, information about LTR hits, and lists of hits by a number of Motifs.

First in ChainP1 comes information about how much this chain is related to the various virus genera. In
this case, it is clearly most related to betaretrovirus.

The next line is empty in this example, but may be of the form
  Broken  |position|>|position|
to indicate that the chain is broken, and where.

101599871
101609336
are the start and end positions of the Chain.

Then follows the virus genus (may be more than one) of the Chain and its total score.

There follow SubGeneHits, each with SubGene name, most similar virus genus and score and its component MotifHits.
Each MotifHit is described by its MotifGroup, virus genus, provenience, score, hotspot position, reading frame and first
and last position. Where applicable, the sequence of the Motif and the DNA sequence (as bases or acids) against which
it was scored are shown.

If there are two LTRs, the estimates of the direct repeats and short inverted repeats at the start and end of the ERV
are shown with the heading 'Integration sites'. If there is only one LTR whose pair companion was in an unacceptable
position there will be instead the heading 'Missing LTR at' and the position of the hotspot of the pair companion.

Chainview displays information in two frames. In the upper (text) frame, the contents of the Chain parameter are
displayed, followed by the DNA sequence corresponding to the Chain.

The lower, graphic frame displays positions in DNA at the top, and the following features, separately for the three
reading frames if applicable:

  • Motif Hits, by a heavy black rectangle. The score is shown inside the rectangle, identifying information below.
  • ALUs and L1s and other transposons detected by SweepDNA as vertical dashed black lines, with the
    identification of the element at the lower end.
  • Breaks as squiggly green lines.
  • Start (methionine) codons as vertical dashed blue lines.
  • Stop codons, as vertical red lines, in upper half (tga codon), lower half (taa) or both halves (tag).
  • Potential sites for N-glycosylation, as vertical short thick green lines.
  • Hits with SlipperyMotif, as small 'S'.
  • Hits with PseudoKnotMotif , an '8'.
  • Hits with SpliceAcceptorMotif , as '\'.
  • Hits with SpliceDonorMotif , as '/'.
  • Puteins as triple lines.
  • EnvTrace as dashed triple lines.
  • XXons as double lines.
  • Xons as single lines.
The first three features are always shown if applicable. The others may be turned off or on using the parameter panel
at the right, clicking or unclicking the relevant boxes. For some reason, changes may not take effect until the cursor is
moved into the graphics frame.

If the cursor is put over a Putein, XXon or Xon, it will be shown in red, and some information about it displayed at the
bottom. If the cursor is anywhere in the graphic frame, the DNA position corresponding (approximately) to its horizontal
position is shown at the bottom. These functions sometimes hang in mysterious fashion, but a mouse click anywhere in
the window will help. Clicking will activate Puteinview with the component,

Clicking the 'Save as file' button saves the graphics to a PNG, EPS or JPEG file, 'Save as file, A4' does the same
but folds the graphics to fit into A4 size.

If there should belong a PeudoGene file (see PseuGID) to this Chain, there will be a button to display it.

Clicking the rectangle denoting an LTR in the graphic frame, details of the LTR will be shown in a window of the same
type as displayed by LTRview.

Only one Chain at a time is displayed. There is a menu(s) to select which.

It is not necessary to close a Chainview window before executing other commands.

Chainview is normally invoked by selecting a Chains file as script, and executing that script. If you run it interactively,
this parameter may be changed:

  • 'InputFile'. The Chains file to display.
