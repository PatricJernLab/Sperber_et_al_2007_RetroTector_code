RetroTector

 

Modifiers


Modifiers are classes within RetroTector.jar which provide a numerical measure of something at any position in a DNA sequence. The 'something' may be very varied.

Several Modifiers express the density of something in the neighborhood of the position.
In distribution-adding modifiers this is achieved through creating an array of real numbers, originally zero, of the same length as the DNA. Then the whole DNA is searched for occurrences of the 'something' and each time it is found, the array elements in that position and its surroundings are incremented according to some distribution, presumably one which peaks at the hit position and decreases away from it.

Gaussian modifiers are a special case of distribution-adding modifiers where the distribution is Gaussian, typically with standard deviation 50. The tails are truncated at 1% of peak amplitude.

A Modifier also provides, on request, the average, max and min of its values. The curves of the values may be displayed using the ModifierCurve executor.

At present, these Modifiers are implemented:
 

  • AllORFModifier
  • CpGModifier
  • GlycSiteModifier
  • GTModifier
  • NonORFHexamerModifier
  • ORFHexamerModifier
  • REndModifier
  • SplitOctamerModifier
  • StopCodonModifier
  • TransSiteModifier

AllORFModifier

This Modifier expresses the score for unaligned fitting in the ORFID algorithm.

CpGModifier

This Modifier expresses the likelihood that the position is the centre of a CpG island. It does this by counting, within +-100 bases from the position, the number of 'g' bases (nG), the number of 'c' bases (nC) and the number of cg pairs (nCG). The value of CpGModifier is calculated as (nC + nG) * nCG / (nC*nG).

GlycSiteModifier

This is a distribution-adding Modifier, with a bilinear distribution stretching over +- 90 bases. The feature it reacts to is an asparagine ('n') followed 6 bases later by serine ('s') or threonine ('t').

GTModifier

A Gaussian Modifier that reacts to the 'gt' base pair.

NonORFHexamerModifier

A Gaussian Modifier that reacts to any of a number of hexamers, listed in NonORFHexamers.txt in Database, which are known to be rare in retroviral ORFs.

ORFHexamerModifier

A Gaussian Modifier that reacts to any of a number of hexamers, listed in ORFHexamers.txt in Database, which are known to be common in retroviral ORFs.


REndModifier

This is a distribution-adding Modifier, with a triangular distribution stretching 9 positions in either direction for each occurrence of an internally defined motif, and multiplies by 2 at 'a' bases and by 4 at 'ca' pairs.

SplitOctamerModifier

This Modifier expresses the density of split octamers known to occur in LTRs.

It is a Gaussian modifier with standard deviation 50. A Gaussian is added around each occurrence of one of a number of split octamers.

The source of the split octamers is the multiparameter 'Octamers' in the parameter file Splitoct.txt in Database, but not all octamers in this are used. A line in this parameter consists of two tetramers, minimum and maximum distance (measured as bases between the tetramers), two measures of specificity (LTR/nonLTR ratio and LTR/Random ratio) and list of virus genera where the split octamer occurs.

At creation of a SplitOctamerModifier, Splitoct.txt is read, and those split octamers which have a value >= 20 in the fifth column, and where the two tetramers are different (since repetitive regions is a considerable error source) are retained.

The DNA is then searched for positions where the first tetramer occurs. If the second tetramer is then found within the prescribed distance range downstream, a Gaussian is added around that position.

StopCodonModifier

This is a distribution-adding Modifier, with the same distribution as GlycSiteModifier. The feature it reacts to is stop codons.

TransSiteModifier

This Modifier expresses the density of transcription sites known to occur in LTRs.

It is a Gaussian modifier with standard deviation 50. A Gaussian is added around each occurrence of one of the transcription sites.

The transcription sites are defined in Transsites.txt in Database. Its multiparameter 'Sites' is a list (with identification numbers) of site sequences. Some of them include ambiguity codes which are treated accordingly. The distinction between lower and upper case letters (less or more conserved positions) is not utilized at present.
