RetroTector

 

AcidMotif


This is an OrdinaryMotif. Instances of this class evaluate similarity between a sequence of amino acids and a DNA sequence starting at the target position.

Its identifying string is 'P' (for protein).

Its activity is built on the sequence of amino acids specified as a string in the MOTIFSTRING field in Motifs.txt. It uses the 21 standard character codes for amino acids and also 'x' for unidentified acids. Uppercase characters denote highly conserved acids and yield a higher score.

Raw score is calculated by summing similarity scores between the amino acids in the MOTIFSTRING and the codons in the DNA, according to the acid matrix. If the character in the MOTIFSTRING is uppercase, its contribution is multiplied by a bonus factor supplied by RetroVID. A stop codon in the DNA yields zero contribution.

The hotspot is at the beginning of the acid sequence.
