RetroTector

 

DNA files

The DNA sequences to be analyzed are presented as this type of Parameter files. Files in other formats may be acceptable initially, since they are converted by SweepDNA .

Base positions larger than 2^31 can not be handled. If this is a problem, perhaps with a large genome not split into chromosomes, RoughcutDNA may be used to cut it into pieces.

There is one required parameter:

  • Sequence. Structure: see below.

There are four optional parameter:

  • Processed. If Yes, indicates that SweepDNA has processed this file, removing ALUs etc.
  • ValidBases. The number of a, c, g or t outside inserts.
  • FirstUnPadded: The position of the first base not faked by SweepDNA, or -1.
  • LastUnPadded: The position of the last base not faked by SweepDNA. or -1.

 Structure of 'Sequence' parameter

This is a multiparameter, whose lines are in effect concatenated into one long line. If that line starts with an integer, the position of the first base represented in the string is set to its value, otherwise to 1. After that, all characters not used as base codes (tcgawyrmknsvdhb) are ignored. t, c, g and a are interpreted as their respective bases while other basecodes are interpreted as unknown.

There may also be inserts indicated, which will be ignored in reading the file, except that their contents are checked to be consistent. They consist of a set of lines as above, bracketed by two lines of the structure [|label|: and :|label], respectively, where the two labels are identical, typically consisting of 'ALU' (ALU in primary strand), 'CALU' (ALU in secondary strand), 'NALU'  (nested ALU, ie ALU containing another insert, in primary strand) or 'NCALU' (nested ALU in secondary strand) followed by a number. Analogously, there may also be 'L1', 'CL1', 'NL1' or 'NCL1' inserts, describing LINE L1 elements, or analogously named inserts resulting from the action of custom Brooms.


According to needs, RetroTector may retrieve the primary or secondary strand of DNA. The contents of 'Sequence' are assumed to represent the primary strand. If the secondary strand is requested, the bases are complemented (unknown base remains unknown) und reverse ordered. The position numbering then starts at the highest position and decreases. Thus, one particular base pair will be assigned the same position number irrespective of strand.
