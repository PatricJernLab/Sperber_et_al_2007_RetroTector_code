RetroTector

 

LTRImprover


This ChainProcessor tries to provide a Chain with a better pair of LTRs, through a fairly time-consuming alignment procedure. It is included in the standard operation of RetroVID.

There are nine parameters, adjustable through Configuration.txt:

  • ProcessorPriority: Priority relative to other ChainProcessors.
    Default: -1.
  • Threshold: The lowest acceptable value for the internal evaluation of an LTR pair. Range is 0>14000. Practical upper limit ca 6000.
    Default: 0.
  • ChainScoreThreshold: Chains with lower score than this are not treated. Setting a very high value will effectively close down LTRImprover.
    Default: 0.
  • LastLeadSubGene: If there is no SubGene hit with score at least 20 before or at this SubGene, the Chain is not treated.
    Default: NC.
  • FirstTrailSubGene: If there is no SubGene hit with score at least 20 after or at this SubGene, the Chain is not treated.
    Default: TM.
  • MaxOutputChains: Dummy, not used.
    Default: 0.
  • MerLength: The shortest identity accepted in preliminary search.
    Default: 11.
  • CheckTime: The time (in seconds) to check for too long execution time. Default: 300.
  • Debugging: If this is Yes, alignment is output to standard output.
    Default: No
The internal procedure is largely the same as in LTRAlign, and the output with Debugging: Yes is similar.

When LTRImprover has worked for CheckTime seconds, an estimate of the total time is made. If it exceeds 3 * CheckTime, LTRImprover exits, with a Warning message.
